ECE190 MIDTERM - SIDE 1

W2: Professional Engineers Act (RSO 1990 c.P.28)

Practice of engineering = planning/designing/composing/evaluating/advising/reporting/directing/supervising requiring eng principles concerning SAFEGUARDING life, health, property, economic interests, public welfare, ENVIRONMENT

W2: Becoming P.Eng (5 Requirements via PEO)

  1. ≥18 years old
  2. Good character
  3. Academic requirements satisfied
  4. Pass National Professional Practice Exam (ethics/law/practice)
  5. ≥4 years engineering work experience

Protected term. Fines: $10K first, $25K subsequent

W4: Good Requirements = C.T.S.

  • Complete: All functions/objectives/constraints
  • Testable: Objectively verifiable, quantified
  • Solution independent: Don't constrain design prematurely

W4: Prototypes vs Models vs Simulations

  • Physical Prototype: One-off functional life-size
  • Physical Model: Small-size, not fully functional
  • Simulation: Computer-based version

Testing: α-test=internal staff, β-test=real users

W2: Team Stages (Tuckman 1965)

Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing

ECE190 MIDTERM - SIDE 2

W5: Sustainable Development (Brundtland 1987)

"Meeting needs of PRESENT without compromising ability of FUTURE GENERATIONS to meet their own needs"

W5: 3 Components (interconnected, need balance)

  • ENVIRONMENTAL: Air/water/land quality, resources, climate
  • ECONOMIC: Living standards, employment, prices, taxes
  • SOCIAL: Security, health, education, fairness

W5: Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)

Stages: Production → Usage → Maintenance → Disposal

Cradle to grave vs Cradle to cradle (circular economy: waste=resource)

W5: 3R's in Engineering

  • REDUCE: Less materials/energy. Encourage via: taxes, laws (right to repair), mindset
  • REUSE: Design for disassembly & reuse
  • RECYCLE: Use recycled materials (asphalt, concrete, plastic, metal)

W6: 6 CENTRAL ETHICAL DUTIES ★

  1. PARAMOUNT duty to SOCIETY & ENVIRONMENT (most important!)
  2. Duty to employers & clients (fairness, competence, confidentiality)
  3. Duty to colleagues & profession (support, uphold reputation)
  4. Duty to avoid/disclose conflicts of interest

    Conflict = derive personal benefit from official actions

  5. Duty to maintain & act with competency (work within competence)
  6. Duty to report (dangers, violations, unethical behavior)

W6: 4 Ethical Perspectives

Utilitarianism, Duty ethics, Rights ethics, Virtue ethics

(Often consistent, sometimes conflict)
